Junseo Lee

I am an incoming PhD student in Quantum Science and Engineering at the Harvard Kenneth C. Griffin Graduate School of Arts and Sciences, starting in Fall 2026.

I am fortunate to be co-advised by Anurag Anshu and Sitan Chen at the John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, as well as Jordan Cotler at the Department of Physics. I will also be part of the Harvard Quantum Initiative and the Theory of Computation Group, and will be cross-registered at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).

I am currently a Research Associate at the SNU Institute of Computer Technology, hosted by Kabgyun Jeong.

My research focuses on the mathematical and theoretical computer science aspects of quantum systems, particularly quantum learning theory and quantum complexity theory, as well as their connections to quantum many-body physics.

Previously, I completed my mandatory military service in South Korea as a Technical Research Personnel and was a Research Affiliate at the SNU Research Institute of Mathematics. I received my B.S. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Yonsei University, supported by the Hyundai Motor CMK Science and Technology Scholarship.
